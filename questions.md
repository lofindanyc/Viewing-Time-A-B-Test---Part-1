## Stakeholder Questions for Workshop Analysis

### 1. Business Objective & Context
- What made you decide to run this experiment on the workshop content?
- How does this fit into the bigger picture for NotGPT’s training goals?
- What kind of decisions are you hoping this analysis will help you make?

### 2. Success Metrics & Definitions
- When you think of a successful workshop, what does that look like?
- Which metrics do you rely on the most to measure that success?
- If some metrics improve while others don’t, how should we decide what matters most?

### 3. Experiment Design & Hypothesis
- What were you trying to learn or test with this experiment?
- What changes were made to the treatment group compared to the control group?
- Before seeing the data, what outcome were you expecting?

### 4. Data Collection & Quality
- How was the data collected, and what tools or systems were used?
- Over what time period was this data gathered?
- Were there any outside factors that might have affected the results?
- Is there anything about the data quality we should be cautious about?

### 5. Content Structure & Assumptions
- How do you usually decide the right length for content (slides, words, or time)?
- What assumptions do you have about how long people stay engaged?
- From your experience, how do people actually go through the workshop content?

### 6. Audience & Segmentation
- Who is the main audience for this workshop?
- Do you expect different groups of learners to respond differently?
- Are there certain behaviors or patterns we should look at across different users?

### 7. Insights & Application
- How will you use the results from this analysis moving forward?
- What kind of output would be most helpful for you (summary, visuals, deep dive)?
- Are there any specific questions you want this analysis to answer?

### 8. Constraints & Timeline
- When do you need the results by?
- Are there any limitations or constraints we should keep in mind?

## Goal Alignment 
Align with stakeholders on what success means before interpreting any numbers.
- What were you hoping to improve or learn when you ran this experiment?
- How do you personally define a “successful” workshop?
- Which metrics do you pay the most attention to, and why do those matter to you?
- Which of those metrics are you directly responsible for?
- If everything went really well, what would the results look like?
- If some metrics improve but others drop, how should we think about that trade-off?
- How do you plan to use the results of this analysis going forward?

## Understanding the Experiment
I want to get a clearer picture of why this test was run and what we expected going in.
- What idea or assumption were you trying to test with this experiment?
- What exactly was changed in the treatment group compared to the other group?
- What were you expecting to happen before seeing the results?
- How closely do these results match what you usually see in real workshops?

## Content & Learning Experience
Since this is about workshop content, I want to understand how you think about structure and engagement.
- What’s your view on the ideal length of content (word count, slide count, or duration)?
- How do you think content length affects how people engage or learn?
- Do you have any assumptions about attention span or reading time?
- From your experience, how do learners typically interact with this type of content?

## Define Over-performance 
I also want to understand what it means if the results are much better than expected.
- What kind of improvement would feel significant or meaningful to you?
- At what point would you feel confident rolling this change out more broadly?
- If one metric improves a lot but others don’t, how would you interpret that?
- Are there any downsides we should watch for if performance increases a lot?

## Data & Context
To make sure the analysis is accurate, I want to understand where the data comes from 
- How was this data collected?
- What time period does it cover?
- Were there any outside factors during that time that could have influenced the results?
- Are there any known issues or gaps in the data?

## Data Sourcing
Finally, I want to see if there’s anything else we can use to strengthen the analysis.
- Are there other data sources that could help us better understand what’s going on?
- Do we have any qualitative feedback, like surveys or learner comments?
- Is there any data you wish you had that would make this analysis more useful?
